Dining Etiquettes

Summary

The lesson outlines the importance of etiquette in our daily and professional lives, emphasizing that etiquette involves guidelines for respectful behavior towards others. Proper etiquette helps in building trust and long-term relationships. Various types of etiquette are discussed, including social, religious, dining, telephone, and corporate etiquette. Dining etiquette is highlighted with specific rules like starting from the outside and working your way in when using utensils, placing napkins on your lap, breaking bread into small pieces, and ensuring not to blow on hot food. Social etiquette is also covered, stressing the importance of first impressions, which include a genuine smile, warm greeting, positive energy, appropriate eye contact, active listening, and proper grooming.

Lessons Learnt

  • Respect for Others: Etiquette involves showing respect to yourself and others, which is essential for harmonious coexistence.
  • Building Relationships: Proper social behaviors help establish trust and can lead to long-term relationships.
  • Dining Etiquette:
    • Start from the outside and work your way in with utensils.
    • Place your napkin on your lap and wait for the host to do so first.
    • Break bread into small pieces and use a personal portion of butter.
    • Always pass salt and pepper together.
    • Take small bites and chew with your mouth closed.
    • Avoid blowing on hot food.
  • First Impressions:
    • Smile genuinely and greet warmly.
    • Maintain positive energy and appropriate eye contact.
    • Listen actively.
    • Dress appropriately and maintain good grooming.
  • Judgment in Seconds: People quickly judge your economic level, education, and social position, making first impressions crucial.
