Portfolio Writing

Summary

Portfolios are used in various contexts, including job applications, career advancement, creative industries, education, and professional development. They can be categorized into personal, career/dossier, employment, and assessment portfolios, each showcasing different aspects of an individual's work, skills, and achievements. Online platforms such as LinkedIn, Behance, and WordPress provide tools for creating and hosting portfolios.

Lessons Learnt

  • Portfolios are not limited to creative fields, but can be used in various industries and contexts.
  • There are different types of portfolios, each serving a specific purpose, and it's essential to understand the differences.
  • Online platforms offer a range of tools and services to create and host portfolios, making it easier to get started.
  • Portfolios can be used for more than just job applications, but also for career advancement, professional development, and personal branding.
  • Having a portfolio can be beneficial for demonstrating skills, achievements, and progress over time.
  • It's essential to choose the right type of portfolio and platform based on individual needs and goals.
  • Portfolios can be used to showcase not only creative work but also professional achievements, credentials, and learning progress.
  • Creating a portfolio requires a clear understanding of its purpose and the audience it's intended for.
