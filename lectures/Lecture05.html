Telephone Etiquettes

Summary

The guidelines outline the importance of telephone etiquette in business, providing basic rules for effective communication. This includes speaking directly into the mouthpiece, being courteous, and answering calls promptly. When placing callers on hold or transferring calls, it's essential to ask for permission and verify with the caller. Taking messages accurately and returning calls professionally are also crucial. The guidelines emphasize the importance of proper language, tone, and closing conversations professionally.

Lessons Learnt

  • Speaking directly into the mouthpiece and being courteous are fundamental to good telephone etiquette.
  • Answering calls promptly, within 2-3 rings, and greeting callers professionally sets a positive tone.
  • When placing callers on hold, it's essential to ask for permission and check back periodically to avoid keeping them waiting.
  • Transferring calls requires explaining the reason, verifying with the caller, and ensuring the receiving party can take the call.
  • Avoid using phrases like "in a meeting" and provide an approximate availability time instead.
  • Taking messages accurately and confidentially using forms is crucial.
  • Returning calls should be done at a specific time and with clear objectives.
  • Using proper language and tone is vital in conveying a positive message to the caller.
  • Closing conversations professionally, avoiding slang and negative language, and offering assistance even if it's not within your expertise is essential.
  • Using appropriate terminology and avoiding unprofessional expressions is important for maintaining a professional image.
